Ignoring the warnings that there weren’t enough fish, the cannery business collapsed in the 1950’s.

Hovden Cannery closed in 1973. In 1984, the cannery found a new purpose as the location of the Monterey Bay Aquarium.

The Monterey Bay Aquarium became one of the most popular aquariums in the US. It brought back life to the area and became a major spot for tourism.

It was the first of its kind to display a kelp forest and a Great White Shark. You can also find sea otters there.

Carrying on the legacy of Ed Ricketts, the aquarium is also a place for research and ocean life conservation.

John Steinbeck legacy would also be honored. In 1958, Ocean View Avenue was renamed Cannery Row after his novel.

Getting the fish from the boats to the sea was a challenge because of the dangerous, rocky shores. Metal buckets carrying 600 pounds of fish were winched up to the canneries by wire cables.

Boats called clippers were used to catch fish. That was until cannery ownder Knut Hovden introduced the purse seiners to his business in 1927. He also replaced the bucket winch system with floating wooden hoppers to accomodate for the bigger catches.
